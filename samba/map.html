Trail Systems

Hershey Medical Center Trails
Go
SAMBA partnered with Penn State University at the Milton S. Hershey Medical Center Campus to develop 10 miles of trails with options for users of all skill levels. Trails can be accessed traffic-free from the surrounding communities using the Eshenour Memorial bike path. The trail system is divided into two distinct areas, East and West sides. The unique terrain is generally rocky and rugged and while the East side offers more open, flowing trails, the West side is more technical and challenging.
Rattling Creek Trail System
Go
The Rattling Creek trail network, awarded IMBA Epic staus in 2012, covers over 20+ miles of rocky challenging classic east coast singletrack terrain, as well as miles of tamer snowmobile trails and gravel forest roads. The trail network encompasses trails on the Weiser State Forest Haldeman Tract and on Lykens Borough property. The trails are located just outside of Lykens, PA. Please remember to always wear fluorescent orange during hunting season. Hunting is permitted 7 days a week during hunting seasons.

Governor Dick
Go
Officially named the ‘Clarence Schock Memorial Park at Governor Dick,’ the park is located in Mount Gretna. The park offers approximately 10 miles of trail open to bicycles. Signs are clearly marked to indicate which trails are open to bicycles, and which are not.

Ephrata Bike Park at Heatherwood
Go
The project was a partnership between SAMBA, Ephrata Borough, Mainspring of Ephrata, Green Mountain Cyclery, and local brewers at St. Boniface Craft Brewing, Black Forest Brewing, and Pour man Brewing Company. The park consists of a beginner trail and an advanced trail totaling 1.5 miles, a pump track, balance bike track for the little ones, two bridges, dirt jumps and three skills areas.

Mission

The purpose of the Susquehanna Area Mountain Bike Association is to protect and encourage mountain bike trail access by creating positive working relationships with area land management agencies. We will provide information, education and support to Central Pennsylvania mountain bike riders.
